How does it work?

Blockchain Technology
This voting system utilizes a blockchain-style database to store user information and votes. Wikipedia describes blockchains as "a continuously growing list of records, called blocks, which are linked and secured using cryptography." This list of records is essentially a ledger of transaction history. Exact copies of the ledger are kept on multiple computers, called nodes, across a wide network. Each computer routinely compares its copy of the ledger against all others on the network as transactions take place. This way the ownership of digital assets is always tracked, so no one can cheat the sytem by "double spending" an asset or lying about the amount of assets they own.
Applying Blockchains to Voting
The blockchain principle previously described can be used to allow voters to cast votes and have them securely stored. The digital asset becomes the vote to be cast, and the ledger becomes a record of votes being sent from voters to respective candidates.

Is it secure?

Inherent Security
Blockchain ledgers are secure by their nature. New blocks of transactions are added to the chain only after a computationally difficult hash algorithm has been solved. All nodes on the network continually attempt to solve this algorithm, and, once having done so, they broadcast their solution to all other nodes who verify it. If the majority of nodes agrees on the solution, the new block is added to the chain and the process begins again with the next new block of transactions. Wikipedia points out, "Once recorded, the data in any given block cannot be altered retroactively without the alteration of all subsequent blocks, which requires collusion of the network majority."
